BOCW Registration

Building & Other Construction Workers (BOCW) Registration

The BOCW Act, 1996 requires all employers involved in construction activities employing 10 or more workers to register under this act and contribute to the Workers' Welfare Fund (Building Cess @ 1% of project cost).

Who needs BOCW Registration?

  • Builders, real estate developers, and construction companies
  • Civil contractors handling government or private projects
  • Infrastructure companies with active construction sites
  • Any employer employing 10+ construction workers

Documents Required:

  • PAN Card of the company / proprietor
  • GST Registration Certificate
  • Work order / Contract agreement copies
  • List of employees with Aadhar and bank details
  • Labour Cess payment receipts (1% of construction cost)
  • Address proof of the establishment

CLRA Registration

Contract Labour (Regulation & Abolition) Act Registration

The CLRA Act, 1970 regulates employment of contract labour by establishments employing 20 or more contract workers. Both the Principal Employer and the Contractor need separate registrations under this Act.

Who needs CLRA Registration?

  • Any establishment employing 20+ contract workers (Principal Employer)
  • Contractors supplying 20+ workers to establishments (Contractor License)
  • Industries like manufacturing, construction, IT parks, hospitals with contract staff

Benefits of CLRA Compliance:

  • Legally operate with contract workforce — avoid penalties up to ₹1,000/day
  • Avoid work stoppages during Labour Department inspections
  • Required for all government tender participation
  • Builds trust with principal employers and clients
PF Registration & Monthly Filing

Provident Fund (PF) Registration & EPFO Compliance

Under the Employees' Provident Funds & Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, every establishment employing 20 or more persons must register with the EPFO. Employer contributes 12% and employee contributes 12% of basic wages.

Who needs PF Registration?

  • Factories, shops, and establishments with 20+ employees
  • Some establishments with less than 20 employees can also voluntarily register
  • All employees earning up to ₹15,000/month (basic) are mandatorily covered

Our Monthly PF Filing Services Include:

  • Monthly ECR (Electronic Challan-cum-Return) preparation and submission
  • UAN generation for new employees
  • Monthly challan generation and payment assistance
  • PF Transfer and withdrawal support
  • Annual return filing (Form 3A & 6A)

ESIC Registration

Employee State Insurance Corporation (ESIC) Registration

ESIC provides medical, cash, maternity, disability, and dependent benefits to employees earning below ₹21,000/month. All factories and establishments with 10 or more employees must register under the ESI Act, 1948.

Benefits ESIC Provides to Employees:

  • Full medical treatment for employee and their dependents
  • Sickness Benefit — 70% of wages during certified illness (up to 91 days)
  • Maternity Benefit — 100% of wages for 26 weeks
  • Disablement Benefit for work-related injuries
  • Dependent's Benefit in case of employee death

Our ESIC Services:

  • ESIC Registration for employer and employees
  • Monthly contribution filing (on or before 15th of every month)
  • ESIC Card facilitation for employees
  • Half-yearly return preparation and filing
Professional Tax

Professional Tax (PT) Registration & Return Filing in West Bengal

Professional Tax is a state-level tax levied by the West Bengal government on all salaried individuals and professionals. Employers must deduct PT from employee salaries and remit to the state government monthly.

Who needs PT Registration?

  • All employers in West Bengal with salaried employees
  • Self-employed professionals (doctors, lawyers, CAs, consultants)
  • Shops, factories, offices, and establishments of all sizes
  • Freelancers and business owners earning above the threshold

PT Slabs in West Bengal (Monthly Salary):

  • Up to ₹10,000 — No Tax
  • ₹10,001 – ₹15,000 — ₹110 per month
  • ₹15,001 – ₹25,000 — ₹130 per month
  • ₹25,001 – ₹40,000 — ₹150 per month
  • Above ₹40,000 — ₹200 per month (₹2,400 per year)

📋 PT Registration & Filing Process

Enrollment CertificateEmployer obtains PT Enrollment Certificate from the West Bengal Commercial Tax Dept.
2
Employee DeductionPT is deducted from employee salary every month based on the applicable slab.
3
Monthly RemittanceDeducted PT is deposited with the state government on or before the 21st of each month.
4
Annual Return FilingWe file the annual PT return on your behalf every year before the due date.
5
Compliance CertificatePT compliance certificate maintained for audits and inspections.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about labour law compliance in Kolkata — answered simply.

CLRA stands for Contract Labour (Regulation & Abolition) Act, 1970. Both the Principal Employer (the company hiring contract workers) and the Contractor (the agency supplying those workers) must register under this Act if 20 or more contract workers are employed at any time. Without this registration, contractors cannot legally operate, and employers risk heavy fines and work stoppages during Labour Department inspections.
Any employer or contractor engaged in building or construction activities employing 10 or more workers is required to register under the Building and Other Construction Workers (BOCW) Act, 1996. This includes builders, real estate developers, civil contractors, road contractors, and infrastructure companies. Failure to register can attract fines and stop-work orders on construction sites.
Yes, PF (Provident Fund) registration is absolutely mandatory under the Employees' Provident Funds & Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952. Any establishment employing 20 or more persons is required to register with EPFO. Once an establishment crosses the threshold, it must remain registered even if employee count later falls below 20. Employers contribute 12% and employees also contribute 12% of basic wages.
ESIC (Employee State Insurance Corporation) is a social security scheme under the ESI Act, 1948. It provides comprehensive medical care, sickness benefit (70% wages for 91 days), maternity benefit (26 weeks at 100% wages), disablement benefit, and dependent's benefit to insured employees earning up to ₹21,000/month. All factories and establishments employing 10 or more employees must mandatorily register under ESIC. The employer contributes 3.25% and employee contributes 0.75% of gross wages.
With all documents in place, most statutory registrations are completed within: PF Registration — 3–5 working days; ESIC Registration — 2–3 working days; BOCW Registration — 5–7 working days; CLRA Registration (Principal Employer) — 7–10 working days; CLRA Contractor License — 10–15 working days; Professional Tax — 3–5 working days. Timelines may vary depending on the volume of applications at the concerned government department at that time.

Workmen Compensation (WC) Insurance is a mandatory insurance policy under the Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923. It protects employers from financial liabilities arising from work-related accidents, injuries, or death of employees. Without a valid WC policy, employers are personally liable to pay compensation — which can run into lakhs of rupees. It is particularly important for construction, manufacturing, logistics, and other high-risk industries.
